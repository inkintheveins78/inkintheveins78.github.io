Bio

Let me tell you a story.

When I was growing up, I always made things. My first love, like many other children, was Lego. I built everything I could imagine, all day. When I was in third grade, I made a play with my classmates, all on our own. In fourth grade, I worked with my friends to make comics. In fifth and sixth, I would draw pictures for my friends and family so they could see a different version of themself. When I got to seventh grade, I discovered my passion for web design and coding after I saw what a few key strokes and some motivation could do. Eventually, I got to making the websites you see below, all of which derive of my desire to constantly create.

I am a creator, not just a web designer





Projects

Below are a few projects that I believe show my skill and abilities best. First, amistuff, which is a way for me to store all my medias, like my youtube channel, blog and my games that I coded in scratch and ruby. Next is my BSA troop's website. I earned the programming merit badge so I was given the position of webmaster, and I made this website after noticing we didn't have one. Lastly is HTMLearn, which is my way to sell a course I made on web design.

I feel that all of these websites are some of my best work, but they have all been consistently updated after learning new design and code concepts, showing how I can learn and adapt.
